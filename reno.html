About Reno

Nestled in the northwest part of the state, Reno is a vibrant city with a population of approximately 273,448 residents. Established as a city on May 9th, 1868 Reno boasts a rich history. Its classification as both urban and suburban underscores its dynamic nature. Reno's average income level is $38,633.00, however, it can often be comparable to state averages.

Quick Facts about Reno

  1. Washoe, a chimpanzee raised in Reno was the first non-human to communicate through sign language and learned over 350 different signs.
  2. Reno experiences around 1000 earthquakes a year.
  3. Reno was named after a man that has never been to Reno. His name was Jesse Lee Reno, a fallen soldier.
  4. Reno is known as the divorce capital of the world after 30,000 couples divorced between 1929-1939.
